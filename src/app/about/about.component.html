What is skatetrax?

When I began figure skating, I started as an adult. I had recently started a new job near a rink and was ready to learn everything there was about skating. When I transistioned from rental skates to my first pair of skates I was told about how maintain them and how important that would be to my success. I didn't know it at the time but that was when skatetrax was actually born. The idea was to simply keep track of how many hours I had skated and then how much it was costing me to skate every week. Back then, it wasn't as easy to keep up with as it is now.

As my skating evolved, so did skatetrax. The idea of keeping records didn't feel like enough and I believe that when every one is able to view historical data, they can make educated decisions about how to plan for what is next. A coach being able to see how much practice a student does on their own, or a skater that can see how much coached time they had last month - these types of trends help every one stengthen themselves for success on the ice. That is what skatetrax aims to do.

Skatetrax will always continue to evolve. We welcome feedback and suggestions at all times and truly hope to have a platform that is easy to use for all. No gimmicks. No distractions.
